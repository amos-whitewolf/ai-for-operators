Case Study 04

Gamification
Design

Needed to make the child-facing side of the app engaging — not just "safety software your parents installed." Designed a whole engagement system with AI.

  • Rebranded child reports as "Gaming Recap" — parents see "Safety Report"
  • Duolingo-style streaks and Xbox-style achievements
  • Stats: talk time, voice count, loudest moment, vibe check
  • Random silly awards + weekly Wrapped-style recap
  • All on-device, no backend — shipped fast
Strategy: make kids want to open the app → guaranteed trial sessions → higher conversion for parents.

The Real Insight

The Communication Tax

30-40% Of team time spent on communication

In a traditional team, the leader often does nothing but communication and strategy. Customer problems go through 3 people to reach the founder. Then 3 people back down to fix it. Then someone handles the comms. Then it gets run by the founders again.

With AI, you see the customer problem, you solve it, you ship the fix, you write the post — all you. No overhead, no lag, no broken telephone.
Implementation

How to actually
start doing this

  1. Give one person a project that's too big for them. Full ownership, end-to-end. Something within their skillset but stretching beyond it. They'll have to figure out AI because it's not possible to do manually.
  2. Let them learn by doing. That person is going to learn more about AI workflows in one real project than a month of courses or demos.
  3. Start removing time sinks for everyone else. Who's getting hit with repetitive work? What eats hours but doesn't need human judgment? That person becomes the one who builds systems to free up the rest of the team.
  4. Think in terms of ownership. People need to think more like founders — own your work end-to-end, pull in resources when needed, don't over-rely on others. One person + AI targeting 300 new customers in a year? That's a project worth owning.
